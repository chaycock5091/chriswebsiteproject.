Describe the purpose of IT in modern business.

Technology is very important in business operations. No matter how large your business is, technology has both tangible and intangible benefits that will help you make money and satisfy your customers by giving them what they demand. Technology affects the efficiency and relationships of your business and also affects the security of confidential information. [1]

Describe the risks that might impact digital projects.

There are many types of risks of concern to projects, these risks can result in unwanted expenses and performance problems for you and your organisation. For example governance risk relates to board and management performance with regard to ethics, community stewardship, and company reputation. Another risk is choosing a technology that can’t be made to work which will cause errors with your project, also with the advancements of technology you will have to keep updated on the new programmes or buy completely new technology. [4]

Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software.

Choosing software for your project is not an easy process, many people know that they will need a new system but can be put off because of competing products, fear of choosing the wrong thing or they don’t have enough time. If you don’t have the resources to spare it is very important to get it right the first time. If I want to make a good decision when choosing new IT systems and software I will follow these 7 steps. [5]

  1. Know your objective.
  2. Create a list of software requirements.
  3. Start researching for relevent applications.
  4. start excluding solutions.
  5. Start trialling your selected software.
  6. Do your due diligence.
  7. Start negotiating.

Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects.

The internet is the main source of information and entertainment, but it is important to realise the number of restrictions on what you can do and cannot do online. There are legal constraints on the uploading and downloading of software and other digital content, including music and videos. Many items are copyright and can incorporate digital rights management, such as restricting the number of times a media file can be copied or converted to another format. When you buy software, you will  normally receive a licence to use it, you do not own the software itself, nor do you have the right to copy it but some types of licence, including freeware and shareware impose fewer restrictions. Describe copyright and other constraints affecting websites.

Copyright and other laws affect websites because it may restrict what you publish, websites fall under this protection because they contain creative elements like written content and images. First you will need to get permission if you want to publish information on your website from another site. Secondly, use your own photographs or pictures you created as they are your own property.

Describe access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them.

There are many issues that can affect people with disabilities when accessing a webpage, like if someone has a hearing disability but the webpage has audio in it. This can be overcome by adding subtitles on the page or by removing the audio and replace it with something else.
